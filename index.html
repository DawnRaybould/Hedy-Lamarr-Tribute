Hedy Lamarr

She is the reason you can easily look at this page wirelessly

Hedy Lamar is best known as a Hollywood film actress from the 1940s, but a lot of people don't know she was also a brilliant mathemetician and inventor. If you enjoy the ease and convenience of WiFi, you have Hedy Lamarr to thank.

  • November 9, 1914 - Hedwig Eva Maria Kiesler was born.
  • Late 1920s - Hedy Lamarr moved to Germany after being discovered by producer Max Reinhardt.
  • Early 1933 - Lamarr starred in first film, Extacy.
  • Early August, 1933 - Married wealthy arms merchant Friedrich Mandl. He was very controlling and would often keep her confined to their castle home. She would go with him to business meetings where other scientists were in attendance. It was her first experience with applied science and technology.
  • 1937 - Fled Germany and her first husband and went to Paris, where she met the head of MGM Studios, Lous B Meyer. He offered her a contract, and she became a major Hollywood movie star.
  • 1940 - Met co-conspirator George Antheil, a composer.
  • Beginning of WWII - Developed a radio guidance system for allied torpedoes, with Antheil. It used spread spectrum and frequency hopping technology to prevent jamming by Axis powers. The US Navy didn’t use it, however, till the 1960s. Its principles are now incorporated by modern GPS, WiFi and Bluetooth technology.
  • August 11, 1942 - Lamar and Antheil obtained a patent for their invention. They presented their ideas to the National Inventors Council, but were rejected.
  • 1953 - 1953 Lamarr became a US citizen.
  • 1958 - Hedy Lamarr starred in her last film, The Female Animal.
  • 1962 - Lamar and Antheil's idea was used in communications systems on US ships sent to blockade Cuba. Their patent by that time had expired.
  • 1997 - Lamar and Antheil are honored with the Electronic Frontier Foundation Pioneer Award. That same year Lamarr is the first woman to receive the BUBBIE Gnass Spirit of Achievement Award, "'The Oscars'” of inventing".
  • January 10, 2000 - Hedy Lamarr dies at her home in Casselberry Florida.
